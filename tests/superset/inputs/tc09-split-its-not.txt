Most people think writing is about vocabulary. It's not. It's about structure. The best writers don't focus on fancy words. They focus on how ideas connect. Every paragraph leads to the next. Every sentence earns its place. That's the real skill.

But many beginners get this wrong. They stuff their writing with big words and complex sentences. They think it makes them sound smart. It doesn't. It makes their writing hard to read.

The secret is simple: write clearly. Say what you mean. Then stop. If you can do that, you're already ahead of most people.
